# IP-BOUNDARY.md
## NovaFuse ERIL Public Reference Surface — Scope & IP Boundary

This repository is intentionally designed as a **public verification surface** — enough material to prove that executable governance can be run, refused, evidenced, and replayed — without disclosing NovaFuse's full institutional governance IP.

## 1) What is included (public)

This repository includes:
- Minimal ERIL/ERI examples demonstrating:
  - **fail-closed refusal** before any side effect [1](https://www.gsma.com/newsroom/wp-content/uploads//NG.133-v1.0.pdf)
  - **evidence artifacts** as required condition of state transition [1](https://www.gsma.com/newsroom/wp-content/uploads//NG.133-v1.0.pdf)
- Public artifact schemas for interoperability:
  - Evidence schema
  - Refusal schema
- A replay/verification demonstration showing how an independent party can validate artifacts.
- Descriptive documentation explaining concepts at a high level.

> Important: running the public verifier or examples is not a certification, regulatory approval, or compliance determination. [2](https://industrial-software.com/wp-content/uploads/AVEVA_SystemPlatform/2023_r2_p01/System_Platform/ReadMe.html)

## 2) What is intentionally omitted (licensed / private)

The following items are **not** published here and are available only under license, NDA, or standards engagement:
- Full ERIL **normative specification** (complete SHALL/SHOULD/MAY corpus)
- Complete governance lifecycle procedures (authoring, approval, change control, revocation operations)
- Full responsibility allocation / liability framework (institutional RACI at scale)
- Certification criteria, thresholds, assessor runbooks, and conformance decision rules
- Proprietary methods, scoring functions, signal decomposition rules, and trade-secret techniques
- Internal standards mapping matrices beyond high-level descriptive summaries

## 5) Non-claims / safety boundary

This repository demonstrates governance execution and evidence production.
It does **not** claim:
- outcome correctness,
- ethical sufficiency,
- clinical safety,
- or regulatory compliance by itself.

Where ERIs touch regulated domains, the public examples should remain tightly bounded and refusal-first, mirroring governed ERI patterns that avoid diagnosis/treatment recommendations and refuse output when safety conditions are not met. [3](https://arxiv.org/html/2507.05201v2)
